# CHAOS Constitution — Task Tracker API

> Behavioral principles for controlled, human-led, agent-orchestrated work on this
> repository. Generated by `chaos:init --guided` on 2026-07-19. Governed subject:
> the **Task Tracker API** (.NET). See [context](context.md), [architecture](architecture.md),
> and the [bootstrap report](bootstrap-report.md).

## 1. Human ownership

Humans make the material decisions; agents do the orchestrated work. Scope,
architecture, contracts, and risk trade-offs are decided by a human and recorded in
the interaction runtime — never guessed in chat. An agent may move fast on mechanical
work, but it stops at every choice only a human should make.

## 2. Evidence before design

No design, proposal, or verdict is produced from assumption when evidence is available.
Inspect the code, the tests, and the specs first. Where evidence is missing, say so and
label it `UNKNOWN` — do not fill the gap with a confident guess.

## 3. No silent assumptions

Every assumption is written down, labelled, and given an owner. An unlabeled assumption
is a defect. If a command must assume something to proceed, it declares the assumption
and caps its confidence accordingly.

## 4. Fact / inference / assumption separation

Every material finding is classified (see the confidence doctrine below). Inference is
never presented as fact; conflicts are never resolved silently.

## 5. Review before execution

Material change is reviewed before it is applied, at a rigor matched to its blast radius
(`--light` / `--standard` / `--strict`). Gates evaluate real readiness, not file
presence.

## 6. ADR / rule / decision compliance

Work respects the recorded decisions, rules, and gates of this workspace. A change that
contradicts an accepted decision must either be rejected or drive an explicit,
human-approved decision to change that posture — with an audit trail.

## 7. No "agents go brrr" without evidence, gates, and human ownership

Autonomy is bounded. Speed is welcome on mechanical work; it is never an excuse to skip
evidence, bypass a gate, or make a material decision on the human's behalf.

## Confidence and knowledge classification doctrine

Every CHAOS judgement, recommendation, approval, verification, gate result, or review
finding in this repository **must** separate what is known from what is inferred, and
must carry confidence metadata. This is a hard rule, not a style preference.

### Knowledge type — exactly one per material finding

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `FACT` | Directly supported by inspected source, tool output, or explicit user confirmation. |
| `INFERENCE` | Reasoned from available evidence, but not directly proven. |
| `ASSUMPTION` | Accepted temporarily because evidence is incomplete. |
| `UNKNOWN` | Material information is missing or could not be inspected. |
| `CONFLICT` | Two or more sources disagree or imply incompatible positions. |

### Confidence level — on every material finding and every verdict

| Level | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `HIGH` | Direct evidence inspected, validation ran or was evidenced, no major unresolved assumptions. |
| `MEDIUM` | Evidence exists but validation is partial, or the conclusion depends on bounded assumptions. |
| `LOW` | Key evidence missing, validation did not run, sources conflict, or conclusion is mostly inferred. |

### Verdict metadata — no bare verdicts

Every final verdict must include:

- `verdict` — the command-specific judgement.
- `confidence` — `HIGH` / `MEDIUM` / `LOW`.
- `evidence_coverage` — `COMPLETE` / `PARTIAL` / `WEAK`.
- `assumption_load` — `LOW` / `MEDIUM` / `HIGH`.

### Hard rules

- No confidence-less verdicts.
- No unlabeled assumptions.
- No inference disguised as fact.
- No silent resolution of conflicts.
- Missing evidence must reduce confidence or block the relevant gate.
- Low-confidence positive verdicts are conditional, not clean approvals.
